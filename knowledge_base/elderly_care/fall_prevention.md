# Fall Prevention for Elderly Care

## Why Falls Matter for Elderly People
- Most common cause of injury in people over 65
- One fall can lead to broken hip (very serious)
- Recovery takes months of physical therapy
- Can lead to loss of independence
- Prevention is much easier than recovery

## Common Reasons for Falls
- Poor lighting in home
- Loose carpets or obstacles on floor
- Bathroom hazards (slippery surfaces)
- Medication side effects (dizziness from blood pressure meds)
- Weak leg muscles or poor balance
- Vision problems
- Inner ear problems
- Dehydration or low blood sugar

## Prevention: Home Environment

### Lighting
- Install bright lights in hallways
- Install night lights in bedroom and bathroom
- Use lamps next to chairs where you sit
- Remove dark corners

### Pathways
- Remove loose rugs and cables
- Keep walkways clear of clutter
- Remove throw rugs (trip hazard)
- Keep frequently used items within easy reach
- Use non-slip mats in bathroom

### Bathroom (High Risk Area!)
- Install grab bars next to toilet
- Install grab bars in shower/tub
- Use non-slip mat in tub/shower
- Consider raised toilet seat
- Install handheld shower head
- Keep soap and shampoo within reach
- Dry floor thoroughly after use

### Stairs and Steps
- Install handrails on both sides
- Ensure good lighting on stairs
- Mark edges of steps with tape
- Remove obstacles from stairs
- Consider ramp instead of steps if possible
- Never use stairs when sleepy or dizzy

### Bedroom
- Keep night light on at night
- Keep phone within reach
- Keep lamp within reach
- Don't keep items on nightstand edge
- Use sturdy furniture to hold onto (not rolling furniture)
- Wear shoes or non-slip slippers

## Prevention: Your Body and Health

### Exercise to Strengthen
- Walking 20-30 minutes daily
- Leg exercises to build strength
- Balance exercises (hold onto wall, stand on one foot)
- Stretching for flexibility
- Tai chi (excellent for balance)

### Vision and Hearing
- Regular eye exams (at least yearly)
- Update glasses/contacts if needed
- Get hearing tested
- Correct hearing loss with aids if needed

### Medication Review
- Ask doctor if any medications cause dizziness
- Be aware of side effects
- Take blood pressure meds as directed
- Take diabetes medication as directed
- Inform doctor of all medications including over-the-counter

### Nutrition and Hydration
- Drink water throughout the day
- Avoid alcohol (increases fall risk)
- Eat regular meals (prevents low blood sugar)
- Include calcium for bone health
- Vitamin D for bone strength

### Footwear
- Wear shoes with non-slip soles
- Avoid slippers without backs
- Avoid high heels or flip-flops
- Ensure shoes fit well and are comfortable
- Avoid going barefoot

## Warning Signs You're at Risk
- Repeated dizziness
- Blurred vision
- Feeling faint or lightheaded
- Weakness in legs
- Loss of balance
- Confusion or difficulty concentrating
- Difficulty walking (shuffling gait)

## If You Fall

### Immediate Steps
1. Don't panic - stay calm
2. Check yourself for injuries
3. If no serious pain, try to get up slowly
4. Call for help if needed
5. Don't try to get up if you're in severe pain

### After a Fall
- Rest and ice any bumps or sprains
- Monitor for bruising or swelling
- Report to doctor within 24 hours
- Have doctor check for broken bones

### When to Seek Immediate Help
🚨 Severe pain anywhere
🚨 Can't move legs or arms
🚨 Hit your head
🚨 Heavy bleeding
🚨 Confusion or loss of consciousness
🚨 Numbness or tingling

- [ ] Check home for trip hazards
- [ ] Install grab bars in bathroom
- [ ] Improve lighting throughout home
- [ ] Review medications with doctor
- [ ] Do daily balance and strength exercises
- [ ] Get vision tested
- [ ] Get hearing tested
- [ ] Wear appropriate footwear
- [ ] Use assistive device if needed (walker, cane)
- [ ] Keep emergency numbers visible

## Key Points
Falls are NOT inevitable with aging. Most falls can be prevented.
Staying active and strong is the best prevention.
Home safety is crucial.
Regular check-ups help catch problems early.
Report any falls to your doctor immediately.
