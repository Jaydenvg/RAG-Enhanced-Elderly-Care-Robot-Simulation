# Osteoporosis and Bone Health Medications

## CALCIUM SUPPLEMENTATION

### Why Important
- Builds and maintains bone density
- Critical for elderly
- Prevents fractures

### Recommended Intake
- Under 50: 1000mg daily
- Over 50: 1200mg daily (women), 1000mg (men initially)

### Sources
- Dairy (milk, yogurt, cheese)
- Leafy greens (some)
- Supplements if diet insufficient

### Supplement Types
- **Calcium carbonate:** With meals
- **Calcium citrate:** With or without food
- Common brands: Caltrate, Citracal

### Side Effects (Supplements)
- Constipation (very common)
- Bloating
- Nausea
- Usually resolve with time

---

## VITAMIN D

### Why Critical
- Helps body absorb calcium
- Needed for bone health
- Especially important for elderly

### Recommended Intake
- Over 50: 600-800 IU daily (higher doses now recommended)
- Many experts recommend 1000-2000 IU daily for elderly

### Sources
- Sunlight exposure (15-30 min daily)
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)
- Egg yolks
- Fortified milk
- Supplements

### Supplement Types
- **Vitamin D2:** Less potent
- **Vitamin D3:** More potent (better choice)

### Deficiency Symptoms
- Weak bones
- Muscle weakness
- Bone pain
- Easy fractures

## BISPHOSPHONATES (Alendronate, Risedronate)

### What Are They?
Slow bone loss and strengthen bones.

### Common Types
- **Alendronate (Fosamx):** Weekly tablet
- **Risedronate (Actonel):** Weekly or monthly
- **Ibandronate (Boniva):** Monthly tablet or IV injection

### How to Take (IMPORTANT!)
1. Take with full glass of water
2. On empty stomach
3. Stay upright 30 minutes after
4. Don't lie down
5. Take with calcium/vitamin D

### Why Important Timing
- Can cause severe throat/esophagus pain if not taken correctly
- Must reach stomach properly

### Side Effects
- Nausea
- Abdominal pain
- Diarrhea or constipation
- Headache
- Rarely: unusual fractures, jaw problems

### Monitoring
- Bone density scans (DEXA) yearly or every 2 years
- Important to ensure medication working

## HORMONE THERAPY

### For Post-Menopausal Women
- **Estrogen therapy:** Slows bone loss
- **Pros:** Good bone protection
- **Cons:** Increased breast cancer, heart disease risk
- **Duration:** Usually short-term only

### Not Recommended for Long-Term
- Use if no alternatives tolerated
- Monitor closely
- Regular mammograms essential

---

## FALL PREVENTION (Critical!)

### Why This Matters
- For osteoporosis patients, falls = fractures
- Hip fractures very serious at elderly age
- Prevention easier than recovery

### Home Safety
- Remove fall hazards
- Adequate lighting
- Handrails in bathroom
- Non-slip mats
- Clear pathways

### Exercise
- Weight-bearing: Walking, dancing
- Balance: Standing on one leg
- Strength: Light weights or resistance bands

### Vision and Hearing
- Regular eye exams
- Updated glasses if needed
- Hearing checks

### Medication Review
- NSAIDs can increase fall risk
- Some blood pressure meds cause dizziness
- Tell doctor about all meds

---

## BONE-HEALTHY LIFESTYLE

### Nutrition
- Adequate calcium (1200mg daily)
- Adequate vitamin D (1000-2000 IU daily)
- Protein (helps bone and muscle)
- Avoid excessive caffeine
- Limit salt

### Exercise
- Daily walking 20-30 minutes
- Balance exercises
- Strength training 2x weekly
- Regular activity helps maintain bone

### Avoid
- Smoking (damages bone)
- Excessive alcohol
- Sedentary lifestyle

### Monitoring
- DEXA bone scans at diagnosis
- Repeat every 1-2 years
- Work with doctor on treatment goals

---

## KEY POINTS
- Calcium and vitamin D essential foundation
- Medications slow bone loss, don't restore
- Fall prevention critical
- Regular monitoring important
- Lifestyle changes important for success
