# Dementia and Cognitive Support Medications

## What is Dementia?
Dementia causes memory loss and thinking problems. It gets worse over time. Medications slow decline but don't cure it.

---

## DONEPEZIL (Aricept)

### What Is It?
Helps preserve brain chemicals that support memory and thinking.

### Why You Take It
- Slows memory loss
- Helps with thinking and attention
- May help with behavior problems
- Most commonly prescribed

### How to Take It
- **Dosage:** Usually 5-10mg once daily
- **When:** Usually at bedtime
- **How:** Swallow tablet with water

### What to Expect
- Takes 2-4 weeks to see effect (if any)
- Doesn't reverse memory loss
- May slow decline
- Results vary person to person

### Common Side Effects
- Nausea (often improves)
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Muscle cramps
- Fatigue

### Important for Elderly
- Watch for heart problems (can slow heart rate)
- May cause dizziness
- Monitor for falls

---

## RIVASTIGMINE (Exelon)

### What Is It?
Similar to donepezil but different mechanism.

### How to Take It
- **Options:** Tablet, capsule, or patch
- **Dosage:** Varies, usually taken twice daily
- **Patch:** Changed daily, easier for some

### What to Expect
- Similar to donepezil
- Slow process, variable results
- Doesn't restore lost memory

### Side Effects
- Similar to donepezil
- Nausea more common initially
- Usually improves over time

---

## MEMANTINE (Namenda)

### What Is It?
Works differently - protects brain cells from too much glutamate (harmful chemical).

### Why You Take It
- Slows cognitive decline
- Helps with thinking and memory
- Often used with donepezil

### How to Take It
- **Dosage:** Usually 20mg daily
- **When:** Usually at bedtime
- **Starting:** Dose increased gradually (important!)

### What to Expect
- Takes several weeks to see effect
- Results vary
- Doesn't cure dementia

### Side Effects
- Dizziness
- Headache
- Confusion (occasionally)
- Constipation
- Usually mild

---

## COMBINATION THERAPY

### Common Combination
- Donepezil PLUS Memantine
- Treats dementia in two different ways
- May work better together than alone

### Starting Combination
- Start one medication
