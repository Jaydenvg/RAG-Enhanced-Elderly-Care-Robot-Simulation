# Common Drug Interactions in Elderly Patients

## CRITICAL INTERACTIONS

### Lisinopril/Enalapril + NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Naproxen)

**What Happens:**
- NSAIDs reduce effectiveness of blood pressure med
- Can harm kidneys
- Especially dangerous in elderly

**Better Alternative:**
- Use acetaminophen (Tylenol) for pain instead

**If You Must Use NSAID:**
- Use shortest duration possible
- Check kidney function regularly
- Take with food
- Ask doctor to monitor

---

### Warfarin (Blood Thinner) + NSAIDs

**What Happens:**
- Both thin blood
- Increased bleeding risk
- Can cause dangerous bleeds

**Never Combine:**
- Aspirin (even low-dose)
- Ibuprofen
- Naproxen
- Anything in NSAID family

**Safer Alternatives:**
- Acetaminophen for pain
- Ask doctor before any pain medication

---

### Metformin + Contrast Dye (From Medical Tests)

**What Happens:**
- Contrast dye (used in CT scans) can harm kidneys
- Metformin can build up
- Causes dangerous condition (lactic acidosis)

**What To Do:**
- Tell doctor/radiologist about metformin
- Usually stop metformin before test
- Resume after kidney function checked

---

### Beta-Blockers + Calcium Channel Blockers

**What Happens:**
- Both slow heart rate
- Can cause dangerously slow heart (heart block)
- Sometimes used together but requires monitoring

**Important:**
- Only combine if doctor explicitly prescribes
- Regular monitoring needed
- Watch for fainting, severe dizziness, shortness of breath

---

### Multiple Blood Pressure Medications

**What Happens:**
- Too much blood pressure lowering
- Dizziness, fainting, falls
- Kidney problems

**Monitor:**
- Check blood pressure regularly at home
- Report low readings to doctor
- May need dose adjustment

---

### Lisinopril/ACE Inhibitors + Potassium Supplements

**What Happens:**
- Both increase potassium
- Dangerous high potassium (hyperkalemia)
- Can cause heart problems

**Never Combine:**
- Check all supplements
- Many salt substitutes have potassium
- Read labels

---

### Grapefruit Juice + Multiple Medications

**What Happens:**
- Grapefruit affects drug metabolism
- Increases drug levels dangerously
- Amlodipine, Simvastatin, others affected

**Affected Medications:**
- Amlodipine (blood pressure)
- Simvastatin (cholesterol)
- Certain other drugs

**Solution:**
- Avoid grapefruit if taking these medications
- No grapefruit juice either
- Pomegranate also problematic

---

### Alcohol + Blood Pressure Medications

**What Happens:**
- Increases dizziness
- Dangerous falls
- May worsen blood pressure control

**Best Practice:**
- Avoid alcohol completely
- If you drink: Only small amounts
- Tell doctor about alcohol use

---

### Alcohol + Diabetes Medications

**What Happens:**
- Increases risk of low blood sugar
- Can cause dangerous hypoglycemia
- May cloud judgment about symptoms

**Best Practice:**
- Avoid alcohol
- If you drink: Eat food first
- Check blood sugar
- Never drink on empty stomach

---

### Alcohol + Pain Medications

**What Happens:**
- Both affect liver
- Can cause liver damage
- Increases overdose risk

**Important:**
- No alcohol with acetaminophen (Tylenol)
- No alcohol with NSAIDs
- No alcohol with opioids

---

## MEDICATION-FOOD INTERACTIONS

### Warfarin (Blood Thinner) + Vitamin K Foods

**What Happens:**
- Vitamin K reduces warfarin effectiveness
- Must maintain CONSISTENT intake
- Sudden changes dangerous

**Vitamin K Foods:**
- Leafy greens (spinach, kale, broccoli)
- Cabbage
- Brussels sprouts

**Important:**
- Eat same amount daily
- Don't suddenly avoid or increase
- Tell doctor about dietary changes

---

## HERBAL INTERACTIONS

### St. John's Wort + Many Medications

**What Happens:**
- Reduces effectiveness of many drugs
- Birth control, blood thinners, heart meds, others

**Drugs Affected:**
- Warfarin (blood thinner)
- Birth control
- Digoxin (heart med)
- Beta-blockers
- Many others

**Important:**
- Tell doctor about ALL supplements
- Include herbs and vitamins
- Don't assume "natural" means safe

---

### Garlic Supplements + Blood Thinners

**What Happens:**
- Both thin blood
- Increased bleeding risk
- Don't combine

**Similar Problem With:**
- Ginger supplements
- Ginkgo biloba
- Fish oil in high doses

---

## SAFETY CHECKLIST

### To Prevent Interactions:
- Tell ALL doctors about ALL medications
- List includes prescription, over-the-counter, supplements
- Use same pharmacy for all prescriptions
- Ask pharmacist about interactions
- Read medication labels
- Ask questions if unsure
- Report any unusual symptoms
- Maintain consistent diet (especially with Warfarin)
- No herbal supplements without doctor approval
- No alcohol without doctor approval

### When to Call Doctor:
- New medication prescribed
- Starting over-the-counter medicine
- Starting supplement or herb
- Unusual symptoms occur
- Medication not working as expected

---

## KEY POINTS
- Most interactions preventable with communication
- Tell every doctor about every medication
- Pharmacy is good resource for questions
- Supplements can be dangerous - ask first
- Consistent diet matters (especially blood thinners)
- When in doubt, ask pharmacist
