# Heart Disease Medications

## ASPIRIN

### What Is It?
Low-dose aspirin thins blood, preventing clots.

### Why You Take It
- Prevents heart attack
- Prevents stroke
- Post-heart-attack prevention

### Common Dose
- 81mg daily (baby aspirin)
- or 325mg every other day

### Side Effects
- Stomach upset (take with food)
- Bleeding (rare at low dose)
- Allergic reaction (very rare)

### Important for Elderly
- Increased bleeding risk
- Monitor for unusual bruising
- Tell doctor about all medications

## STATINS (Lipitor, Crestor)

### What Are They?
Lower cholesterol to reduce heart attack risk.

### Common Statins
- Atorvastatin (Lipitor)
- Rosuvastatin (Crestor)
- Simvastatin

### How to Take
- Usually once daily
- Often in evening
- Take consistently

### Side Effects
- Muscle pain (tell doctor)
- Nausea
- Headache
- Usually mild and rare

### Important
- Take consistently for best effect
- Don't stop without doctor approval

---

## NITRATES (Nitroglycerin)

### What Are They?
Open blood vessels to improve blood flow to heart.

### Uses
- Angina (chest pain) relief
- Angina prevention
- Heart failure

### Types
- **Tablets:** Under tongue for quick relief
- **Sprays:** Quick relief
- **Patches:** Long-acting, worn on skin
- **Long-acting tablets:** Daily prevention

### For Angina Attack
1. Stop activity
2. Sit or lie down
3. Take nitroglycerin under tongue
4. Wait 5 minutes
5. If still painful, call 911

### Important
- Always carry quick-relief form
- Don't combine with certain ED drugs (dangerous!)
- Replace supplies before expiration

---

## ANTICOAGULANTS (Blood Thinners)

### What Are They?
Prevent blood clots (dangerous for heart patients).

### Types
- **Warfarin (Coumadin):** Requires INR monitoring
- **Direct Oral (Xarelto, Eliquat):** No monitoring needed
- **Injections (Lovenox):** Short-term use

### Important
- Don't miss doses
- Consistent diet with Warfarin
- Bleed easily - watch for bruising
- Tell all doctors you take blood thinner

---

## KEY POINTS
- Multiple medications often needed
- Take consistently as prescribed
- Report chest pain immediately
- Monitor for side effects
- Lifestyle changes also critical
