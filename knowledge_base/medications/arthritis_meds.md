# Arthritis Pain Medications

## What is Arthritis?
Arthritis causes joint pain and inflammation. Common in elderly people. Medication reduces pain and improves movement.

---

## ACETAMINOPHEN (Tylenol)

### What Is It?
Pain reliever that's safest for elderly patients.

### Why You Take It
- Relieves pain
- Gentle on stomach
- Safe for long-term use (with caution)

### How to Take It
- **Dosage:** Usually 500-1000mg every 4-6 hours (max 3000-4000mg/day)
- **Elderly:** Usually max 3000mg/day
- **How:** Swallow tablets with water

### Side Effects
- Usually very few
- Very rarely: liver damage (if you exceed maximum dose)

### Important
- DON'T exceed maximum daily dose
- Tell doctor if you take other medications with acetaminophen
- Avoid if you have liver disease

---

## NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Naproxen)

### What Are They?
Stronger pain relievers that also reduce inflammation.

### Common NSAIDs
- **Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin):** 200-400mg every 4-6 hours
- **Naproxen (Aleve):** 220-550mg once or twice daily

### IMPORTANT FOR ELDERLY
⚠️ **NSAIDs have serious risks for elderly patients:**
- **Stomach problems:** Ulcers, bleeding (major issue)
- **Kidney problems:** Can harm kidney function
- **Heart problems:** Can increase heart attack risk
- **Interact with blood pressure meds:** Reduces effectiveness

### When NSAIDs OK for Elderly
- Only short-term use (a few days)
- With food to protect stomach
- With doctor approval
- Only if kidneys are healthy

### Safer Alternative
**Use acetaminophen instead!** Usually safe and effective for chronic arthritis pain.

---

## TOPICAL CREAMS & GELS

### What Are They?
Rub on skin over sore joint. Reduces pain without whole-body effects.

### Common Products
- Capsaicin cream (derived from hot peppers)
- Menthol cream
- NSAIDs in cream form (Voltaren gel)

### Advantages
- Applied directly to joint
- Few side effects
- Don't affect stomach or kidneys
- Can combine with other medications

### How to Use
- Apply thin layer to skin over joint
- Massage gently
- Can use 3-4 times daily
- Wash hands after (unless treating hands)

### Side Effects
- Skin irritation (rare)
- Warm or burning sensation (normal)

### Best For
- Hand arthritis
- Knee arthritis
- Shoulder arthritis
- Chronic pain management

---

## ARTHRITIS PAIN MANAGEMENT STRATEGY

### First Line (Safest)
1. Acetaminophen regularly
2. Topical creams as needed
3. Exercise and physical therapy

### Second Line (If Above Not Enough)
- Short-term NSAIDs with food
- Talk to doctor first
- Monitor for side effects

### Third Line (Severe Cases)
- Steroid injections
- Stronger prescription medications
- Specialist consultation

---

## SAFETY GUIDELINES FOR ELDERLY

✓ **DO:**
- Take lowest effective dose
- Use acetaminophen first
- Use topical creams
- Do gentle exercises
- Apply heat/ice
- Maintain healthy weight

❌ **DON'T:**
- Take NSAIDs regularly without doctor approval
- Exceed acetaminophen dose limits
- Take multiple pain medications at once
- Ignore stomach pain or indigestion
- Stop all activity (keep moving!)

---

## WHEN TO CALL DOCTOR
- Pain not relieved by medication
- Stomach pain or nausea with NSAIDs
- Swelling getting worse
- New joint swelling
- Difficulty moving

## KEY POINTS
- Acetaminophen is safest for elderly
- NSAIDs have serious risks - use short-term only
- Topical creams are effective and safe
- Exercise is very important
- Weight management helps
- Tell doctor about all pain medications
