# Diabetes Medications

## What is Diabetes?
Diabetes is a disease where your body can't control blood sugar (glucose) levels properly. High blood sugar damages blood vessels and nerves. Medication helps keep blood sugar at safe levels.

---

## METFORMIN

### What Is It?
Metformin is a pill that helps your body use insulin better and reduces how much glucose your liver makes.

### Why You Take It
- Controls blood sugar
- Most common diabetes medication
- Safe for elderly patients
- Helps prevent heart disease

### How to Take It
- **Dosage:** Usually 500mg to 1000mg twice daily
- **When:** With meals (reduces stomach upset)
- **How:** Swallow whole with water

### Common Side Effects
- Nausea or upset stomach (usually improves)
- Metallic taste in mouth
- Diarrhea or constipation

### Serious Side Effects (Very Rare)
🚨 **Lactic acidosis** (builds up in blood)
- Severe weakness
- Difficulty breathing
- Seek immediate help

### Important for Elderly
- Monitor kidney function regularly
- Less effective if kidneys not working well
- Don't skip doses

---

## INSULIN

### What Is It?
Insulin is a hormone that helps cells use glucose. Diabetes patients may need insulin injections.

### How to Use It
- **Injection:** Usually in belly or thigh
- **Timing:** Usually before meals or at bedtime
- **Dose:** Varies per patient (doctor determines)

### Side Effects
- Low blood sugar (hypoglycemia) - MOST IMPORTANT
- Weight gain
- Bruising at injection site

### HYPOGLYCEMIA WARNING SIGNS (CRITICAL!)
🚨 Shakiness
🚨 Sweating
🚨 Fast heartbeat
🚨 Anxiety or nervousness
🚨 Blurred vision
🚨 Confusion
🚨 Slurred speech
🚨 Loss of consciousness

**IF YOU HAVE THESE SIGNS:**
1. Stop what you're doing
2. Check blood sugar if possible
3. Eat 15g of fast-acting carbs (juice, candy, glucose tablet)
4. Wait 15 minutes, check again
5. If still low, eat another 15g
6. Tell doctor if happens frequently

## GLP-1 AGONISTS (Ozempic, Trulicity, etc.)

### What Are They?
Injectable medications that help body make more insulin when needed.

### How to Use
- **Injection:** Once weekly, usually in belly or thigh
- **Easy:** Comes in pre-filled pen

### Benefits
- Once weekly (easy to remember)
- Helps with weight loss
- Reduces heart disease risk

### Side Effects
- Nausea (usually improves)
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Loss of appetite

---

## MANAGING BLOOD SUGAR

### Monitor Regularly
- Check blood sugar as directed by doctor
- Keep a log of readings
- Share log with doctor

### Signs of High Blood Sugar (Hyperglycemia)
- Excessive thirst
- Frequent urination
- Blurred vision
- Fatigue
- Headache

### When to Seek Emergency Help
- Blood sugar very high (over 400) with confusion
- Severe dehydration
- Fruity-smelling breath (diabetic ketoacidosis)
- Loss of consciousness

### Diet and Exercise
- Regular meals help stabilize blood sugar
- Walking 20-30 minutes daily helps
- Avoid sugary drinks and foods
- Include vegetables and whole grains

---

## KEY POINTS
- Take medication exactly as prescribed
- Check blood sugar regularly
- Know hypoglycemia warning signs
- Seek help if symptoms persist
- Work with doctor on blood sugar goals
