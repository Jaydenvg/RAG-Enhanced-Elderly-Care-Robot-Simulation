# Blood Pressure Medications (Hypertension)

## What is High Blood Pressure?
High blood pressure (hypertension) means the force of blood pushing against artery walls is too strong. This damages your heart and blood vessels. Taking medication helps protect you from heart attack and stroke.

---

## LISINOPRIL (ACE Inhibitor)

### What Is It?
Lisinopril is a pill that relaxes your blood vessels, making it easier for blood to flow. This lowers your blood pressure and protects your heart and kidneys.

### Why You Take It
- Reduces high blood pressure
- Protects your kidneys
- Protects your heart
- Reduces risk of stroke and heart attack
- Especially important for elderly people

### How to Take It
- **Dosage:** Usually 5mg, 10mg, or 20mg once daily
- **When:** Take at the same time every day (morning often works best)
- **How:** Swallow the pill whole with a glass of water
- **With food:** Can take with or without food
- **Important:** Take even if you feel fine - the medication is working

### What to Expect
- Takes 2-4 weeks to see full effect
- Most side effects appear in first 2 weeks then go away
- You won't feel the medication working - that's normal

### Common Side Effects (Usually Harmless)

**Dry cough (10-15% of people)**
- Most common side effect
- Harmless but annoying
- Usually goes away if you stop taking it
- Tell your doctor if it bothers you

**Dizziness when standing up**
- Happens to some people
- Get up slowly from bed or chair
- Hold onto something when standing
- Drink more water
- Usually goes away after a few days

**Tiredness or weakness**
- Usually mild
- Often improves with time

**Headache**
- Usually mild
- Usually goes away

### Serious Side Effects (Call Doctor Immediately!)

🚨 **Severe swelling of face, lips, tongue, or throat**
- This is a severe allergic reaction
- Can happen anytime, even after taking it for months
- Stop taking and get help immediately

🚨 **Severe dizziness or fainting**
- Can be dangerous
- Tell your doctor immediately

🚨 **Difficulty breathing**
- Seek immediate medical help

### Medications to AVOID With Lisinopril

❌ **Potassium supplements** - Can cause dangerously high potassium
❌ **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen, naproxen, aspirin) - Can harm kidneys and reduce effectiveness. Use acetaminophen (Tylenol) instead for pain
❌ **Potassium-based salt substitutes** - Dangerous combination

### Special Information for Elderly People
- May need lower doses than younger people
- Higher risk of dizziness - be extra careful walking
- Increased fall risk - use handrails, walk carefully
- Check blood pressure regularly at home if possible
- Stay well hydrated (drink plenty of water)
- Sit down if you feel dizzy

### If You Miss a Dose
- Take it as soon as you remember
- UNLESS it's almost time for your next dose
- Then skip the missed dose
- NEVER take double doses

### Important Questions to Ask Your Doctor
- "Is this dose right for me?"
- "Can I take over-the-counter pain relievers?"
- "How often should I check my blood pressure?"
- "What blood pressure numbers should I aim for?"
- "What should I do if I get a bad cough?"

## AMLODIPINE (Calcium Channel Blocker)

### What Is It?
Amlodipine is a pill that relaxes your blood vessels by blocking calcium. This makes it easier for blood to flow and lowers your blood pressure.

### Why You Take It
- Lowers high blood pressure
- Reduces workload on your heart
- Prevents chest pain (angina)
- Reduces risk of heart attack

### How to Take It
- **Dosage:** Usually 2.5mg, 5mg, or 10mg once daily
- **When:** Usually in the morning with breakfast
- **How:** Swallow whole with water
- **With food:** Can take with or without food
- **Important:** Don't chew or crush the pill

### What to Expect
- Works within 1-2 days
- Full effect in 2-4 weeks
- You won't "feel" it working - that's normal

### Common Side Effects

**Swelling in feet and ankles** (5-10% of people)
- Usually mild
- Most common side effect in elderly
- Tell your doctor - may be able to switch medication
- NOT the same as a heart problem

**Headache**
- Usually mild
- Usually goes away

**Dizziness**
- Can happen when standing up
- Get up slowly, hold onto something

**Flushing** (face turning red)
- Usually temporary
- Not dangerous

### Special Information for Elderly People
- Effective and generally well-tolerated
- Watch for swelling in feet and ankles
- May have lower fall risk than ACE inhibitors
- Still check blood pressure regularly

## METOPROLOL (Beta-Blocker)

### What Is It?
Metoprolol is a pill that slows your heart rate and reduces how hard your heart works. This lowers blood pressure and protects your heart.

### Why You Take It
- Lowers blood pressure
- Protects heart from overwork
- Prevents chest pain (angina)
- Can help with irregular heartbeat

### How to Take It
- **Dosage:** Usually 25mg, 50mg, or 100mg
- **When:** Usually with breakfast and/or dinner
- **How:** Swallow whole with water
- **Important:** Don't stop abruptly - must taper off with doctor

### What to Expect
- Works within 1-2 hours
- Full effect in 1-2 weeks

### Common Side Effects

**Fatigue/tiredness** (very common)
- Often improves with time
- Tell doctor if severe

**Dizziness or lightheadedness**
- Get up slowly
- Hold onto handrails

**Shortness of breath with exercise**
- Tell your doctor immediately

**Slow heartbeat**
- Normal effect of medication
- Doctor will monitor

**Cold hands and feet**
- Circulation effect
- Usually not serious

### Important Note for Elderly
- Can cause more dizziness than other blood pressure meds
- Higher fall risk
- Be extra careful with walking and climbing stairs
- May affect balance

### Important: NEVER stop abruptly
- Stopping suddenly can cause heart attack
- Must taper down slowly (with doctor)
- Ask doctor before stopping

## WHEN TO CALL YOUR DOCTOR
- Persistent dry cough (lisinopril)
- Severe or worsening dizziness
- Fainting or feeling faint
- Severe headache
- Chest pain
- Shortness of breath at rest
- Severe swelling in feet (amlodipine)
- Signs of infection (fever, fatigue)

## WHEN TO SEEK EMERGENCY HELP (Call 911)
- Chest pain or pressure
- Severe shortness of breath
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe allergic reaction (swelling of face/throat)
- Severe dizziness with inability to walk

## STORING YOUR MEDICATION
- Keep in original bottle with label
- Store at room temperature
- Keep dry - don't store in bathroom
- Keep away from children and pets
- Check expiration date regularly

---

## SUMMARY
Taking blood pressure medication is a lifelong commitment for most people. The goal is to prevent heart attack and stroke. Don't skip doses even if you feel fine. Your body may take several weeks to adjust. Report any concerns to your doctor.
